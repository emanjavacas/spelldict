For on hym was all my loue and my desyre set wyllynge hym to se. And hopynge that the euen before eester he wolde come to me and besyly sate prayenge my prayers abode hym. And than sodeynly after the sonne goynge to rest herde a grete noyse of people in the cyte cryenge as wood people and whan Mary Ihesus moder syttynge my selfe alone knewe not the cause of the grete cryenge rennynge of the people togyder than sayd these wordes to my selfe wolde to god were with my sone Ihesus. who shall tell me ony tydynges of my swete sone Ihesu for drede me sore that ony thynge of hardnes befall vnto hym for haue herde not longe here before that the Iewes haue co~spired cast amonge them his deth. And than Mary sory full sore aferde in this maner sate. And loked yf ony of his apostles wol+de brynge to me ony tydynges of my swete sone Ihesu. herde ano+ne sodeynly one smyte at my dore and rose anone and ranne to the fenester of my chaumbre loked out. And whan sawe Mary maudeleyn clothed in blacke all be+wepte her here adowne aboute her eyen sayd to me these wordes. Come downe to me moost deuoute of all women mayde yt lyest there hydde moder berauysshed of thy sone. And than Mary smyten with the swerde of sorowe went downe dyde open my dore anone the deuoute maudeleyne cryed to me and sayd. reuerende moder moost reuerende of all women knowe ye ony tydynges of Ihesu your swete sone and my reuerende mayster. And than mary moder of Iesu full sory sayenge to her knowest thou ony tydynges maudelayn of my swete sone Ihesu. And than maudelayn all be wepte cryed to me sayd these wordes. Your sone your loue my mayster is now taken with cordes bounden and wyckedly cruelly of the Iewes beten drawen. And whan mary herde this was anone smyten with the swerde of sorowe thrugh the herte fell downe anone to the erth as a deed woman. And whan these tydyn+ges were brought to me it was the begynnynge of the nyght the derkenesse came aboute me that wyst ne+uer wheder went mannes helpe had none but as laye al that nyght on the erth wepy~ge cryenge that heuen myght be fylled therwith all my hous wette with my wepynge of myn eyen. And than sayd a holy fader where are thy behestes why wolde yu ordeyne me to be a moder make me ryche with a chylde now am bereued of my chylde am lefte alone moost vnworthyest of all women. au~gell Gabryell where is now yt ylke blysse that yu behete me where is now ye somnes of grace thou behote. gabryell why woldest thou scorne me moost vnworthyest of all moders. Beholde now Ga+bryell for the Ioye that yu behete me now haue payne for the gladnes now haue sorowe the moderhede am bereued of my chylde for the grace haue sha+me for the lyfe haue deth for the blessynge that yu behete me now is come curse vpon me than sayd to my selfe. vnblessyd moder sorowful moder why wol+dest yu coueyte a chylde and bere a chylde and nourysshe a childe sodeynly wyckedly arte bereued of thy childe with these wordes and wepynge sorowes lamentacyons wepynge of teres spente ye nyght whan tyme the daye began to sprynge the derknes parted awaye than spronge a derknes for me than rose vp fro the erth as had ben almoost deed faylynge all mannes helpe than came to me holy women of galyle that deuoutly had ben in the temple ye nyght in theyr prayers And whan they herde that my sone was take cruelly bou~de wt the seruauntes of ye bysshop to me vnblessyd moder the holy women came with haste me to comforte And than sayd to Mary maudelayne to my systers and to the holy women of Galyle. Goo we now anone that we may se my sone Ihesu that is onely the co~forte of my lyfe and than myght not go for feblenes of my body soo sore had beten it on the nyght before of my swete sone Ihesu but as the holy women my systers susteyned me vp in theyr armes than met wt some of my sones dyscyples wepynge to whome sayd thus Se ye not my swete sone Ihesu. We sawe hym bou~de with cordes beten with scourges his face defouled with spyttynge and ledde forth with wycked seruau~tes of Cayphas to be denyed vnder Pylate and his lokynge was pale and his chere was ghostly all his body chaunged that vnnethes we myght knowe hym. And than Mary Ihesus moder moost soryest of all moders and fulfylled with sorowe sayd these wordes. Ihesu my swete sone what here of the what bytter and harde tydynge be tolde to me of the. May se my swete sone Ihesu in ony maner that myght haue hym out of theyr handes. And they that sawe me make this sorowe sayd to me. Goo lady and tary not yf ye wyll speke with your sone on lyue for now he is lad with armed knyghtes in to Pilates palays for ye Iewes thynke to dampne hym to the moost dyspyteous dethe. And whan Mary herde these wordes was smyten euen thrugh ye herte with sorowe as a deed woman went forth was borne vp with my systers vnnethes myght come to Pylates palays for faynt. And whan came and wolde haue gone in to the palays myght not come nye the gates for the multytude of people but as nygh as myght put me there stode as a stone stycked in the grou~de than cast vp myn eyen to the fo+nester of the palays yf myght haue seen my swete sone Ihesu and than to the fenestre of the palays came Pylate sayd to all the people. fynde no cause in Ihe+su why that he sholde be put to deth wheder ye wyl that Ihesu go or barabam that is a mansleer. And whan herde this lyfte vp myn herte as though had be rered fro deth to lyfe hoped yt barabam mansleer sholde haue be put to deth my sone Ihesu let go on lyue. But than herde an horryble voyce of al the people cryenge and sayd. Do Ihesu on the crosse do Ihesu on ye crosse. was smyten with the swerde of sorowe as a deed woman fell vpon the erth semynge to the people yt had ben deed so laye longe tyll my systers toke me vp com+forted me so stode longe there and abode yf that myght haue my swete sone Iesu or that myght haue spoken to Pylate that he wolde haue delyuered my swe+te sone Ihesu that lothles lambe the wycked Iewes whan they herde me crye and sawe me wepe sore they blamed me sore badde me holde my peas thou theues moder nouryce to this traytour: for thy sone is worthy to be deed for he deceyueth the people therfore yu shalt se hym soone done on the crosse before thyn owne eyen. And than anone fell downe as a woman fallen in dys+payre thus despysed of the people. And anone herde a voyce of ye people cryenge as they had be~ wood sayd. Brynge out to vs Ihesus of nazareth that he were done on the crosse. And anone pylate assented to them fyrst they bette hym wt scourges tha~ clothed hy~ in purpure afterwarde toke hym to the Iewes to spyll on ye crosse And than they brought forth before the eyen of ye wretched moder Ihesu my swete sone crowned wt a crowne of thorne vpon his heed his eyen all pale his face all rede of blode his heere of his heed hangynge ouer his eyen all bebledde berynge a crosse vpon his backe that he sholde dye on a rope aboute his necke as a comyn thefe put bytwene two theues. And whan sawe this cruell syght than fayled of my strength than wexed my sorowe newe whan sawe hym for moche people myght not come nere hym cryed to hy~ he myght not here me for the people that folowed hym. And than sayd my systers to me go we mary by this way for this is the nere waye than we shall mete with your sone speke with hym or he dye than rose vp as a woman strengthed with a newe spyryte and wente fast on that sodeynly mette with my sone Ihesu in the way beringe the crosse vpon his backe than sayd wordes to my swete sone Ihesu. my swete sone Ihesu wheder goost thou so fast so heuyly charged wt that crosse what thynkest thou to do with thy moder to let me thus alone in dyspayre thy~kest thou to forsake thy moder. my dere sone Ihesu take me to thy moder that crosse shall bere it vpon myn owne backe and sone dye thou not without thy moder ne go yu not without thy moder but swete sone Iesu let vs lyue togyder and dye togyder than my swete sone Ihesu hauynge more ruthe on his owne moder than on his owne payne that he suffred anone for sorowe he felle downe vnder his crosse that he bare anone for sorowe of my swete sone Ihesu fell downe as a woman that had gyuen vp her last spyryte there were we bothe all to troden of the people my sone Ihesu co~strayned to aryse go forth with his crosse the wycked Iewes and cruell me the moder of Ihesu vyolently departed fro my sone euerlaye my sone vnder ye crosse. And ye wycked Iewes co~strayned a man we called hym Symeon to bere the crosse to a place was called Caluarye than the cruell Iewes smote my sone Ihesu with theyr fete bete hym with scourges made hymselfe bere it vpon his backe vp at the mou~t of caluarye. And thus hyed the wycked Iewes to offre vp that lothles lambe. And without all other that was my swete sone Ihesu than Mary moost sorowfull of all moders pursued after my swete sone Ihesu as fast as myght to se what deth ye lambe sholde suffre that was my solace my Ioye vnnethes myght come to the mount of caluarye but as was susteyned by my systers so wery ful of woo was in my body by the tyme that came to the mou~t of caluary the wycked Iewes had done my sone vpon the crosse arered vp the crosse and put it in the erth than loked vpon my swete sone Ihesu wt my wepynge eyen byt+terly wepynge cryenge sayd to hym these wordes. my swete sone Ihesu a my moost loue Ihesu why lo+kest thou not vpon thy sorowfull moder why spekest yu not to thy sorowfull moder why wylte yu leue me thus alone wheder shall go my swete sone Ihesu in what hous shall rest my swete sone Ihesu. my swete sone thoughe thou haue no ruthe on thy selfe haue ruthe on thy sorowfull moder. And whan my swete sone Ihesu herde me thus crye wepe he caste his eyen vpon me vnnethes he sayd to me these wordes. Woma~ be of good comforte for herefore came in to this worlde herefo+re toke this body of the yt here hangeth on the crosse to daye for to helpe mannes soule to bye the soules out of payne that were lost for synne therfore suffre this cruell harde passyon that thou seest therfore moder rest now of thy wepynge of thy cryeng for this is my faders wyl also moder let it be thy wyll so in dyenge shall slee the deth wt the vyctorye of my passyon shall ryse the thyrde day. And herefore moder take here that meane tyme Iohan my dyscyple that loue well let hym be thy sone be thou his moder to hym take the to warde for now shall dye on the crosse. And whan my sone Ihesu had sayd these wordes he cast vp his eyen to heuen betoke to his fader his soule and soo with a grete crye yelded vp his ghost than Marye fell downe vpon the erth all the people wende had ben deed. And aboute ye houre of none there came cruell knyghtes and stode before my sone one of them with a spere opened his syde and claue his herte in two and that sorowe clefte my herte in two. And whan my sones syde was opened and his herte clouen a two than wente the knightes theyr way. And than came Ioseph of arinathye a noble man and a ryghtfull and wolde ha+ue taken downe my swete sone Ihesus of the crosse whan sawe hym wexed quycke agayne in my spyryte and toke strength to me and sayd. my swete Ioseph wylte thou take downe the body of my swete sone Ihesu. Now praye the swete Ioseph take hy~ downe and delyuer hym to his wretched moder that deed body of my sone. ho+ly lady and aboue all other blessyd and euer holy. moder and mayde and moder without wemme abyde a lytell whyle and leue thy wepynge and thy sorowe. And byleue that thy sone wyll aryse from deth to lyfe within a shorte tyme. And therfore worthy lady let vs now in ye meane tyme worthely burye this holy body for to morowe is holy daye we may not werke. And whan Ioseph had sayd these wordes to me was somwhat com+forted therwith helped to wasshe my sones body that was defouled with spyttynge betynge bledynge. And whan we had wasshed it we wyped it anoyntes it so at the last was smyten with a newe sorowe than sayd these wordes to my swete sone there he laye deed. clene flesshe vnwe~med that lyest here that were of my flesshe why woldest thou thus dye on the crosse be offred for synne for thou arte holy flesshe clene from all maner of synne and thou hast sore bought the synne of all men. And whan had sayd these wordes fell dow+ne vpon the body of my swete sone Ihesu wepynge byt+terly and cryenge sore. And than kyssed the woundes of his heed than his handes and than his fete. And than the wou~de in the syde and than cleped all the bo+dy in myne armes and kyssed it and sayd these wordes. my swete sone Ihesu the wretched moder wende neuer to haue seen this sorowe of the nor these sorowes haue suffred for the but wende for to haue had many Ioyes neuer to haue departed fro the. And whyle sayd these wordes Ioseph his felawes hyed the~ lyghtly to wynde bynde my sone in a cloth. And whan they had bounde that one partye of his body wolde bynde that other fell downe to the wou~des that were bou~de and vnbounde them agayne efte agayne than was smyten with a newe sorowe that myght not suffre hym to by~de hym of a longe tyme for my sorowe for the lamentacyon that made vnneth myght they all yt stode there take ye deed body fro me so at the laste they wolde bere hym to the sepulture. folowed hy~ wepy~ge cryenge wonderly sore whan they came to his sepul+cre they wolde haue buryed hym anone myght not suffre that in no wyse but mekely prayed them in this maner sayd. ye noble men ye holy women burye not my swete sone Ihesu but suffre me to haue my sone a lytel whyle in myn armes that may kysse hym and whan they sawe the grete sorowe that was in they made grete lamentacyon abode a lytell whyle so that at the last they wolde nedes burye hym than cryed sayd burye me with hym. may not lyue without him And than Ioseph his felawes departed me worshypfully fro the sepulcre honestly worshypfully buryed my sone Ihesu whan he was buryed stode without the tombe wepynge cryenge fulfylled all wt sorowe sayd these wordes. aungell Gabryell yu saydest to me hayle Mary full of grace beholde. am now full of so+rowe thou saydest to me our lorde is wt the and beholde now my lorde my swete sone is put awaye fro me that may not se hym. And also yu saydest to me blessyd be yu amonge all women am turmented cursed. And at the laste thou saydest to me blessyd to the fruyte of thy wombe beholde now my sone that is the fruyte of my wombe is here wyckedly stayne and now lyeth here in tombe full of wou~des. And whan had sayd these wor+des fel downe for sorowe vpon the erth than Iohn~ that was charged of my sone on the crosse to be my sone sawe me thus sorowe he toke me vp in his armes for feblenesse of my body myght not stonde but as Iohn~ other women ladde me in to Iherusalem as went turned my hede oft agayne for sorowe that was de+parted fro ye sepulcre of my swete sone Ihesu all that sawe me in the way were tempted to wepe for ye sorowe lamentacion that made than Iohn~ led me home in to my chambre sayd to me these wordes. Now rest here the moder of my lord vpon ye arysynge of the swete sone Ihesu and my lorde seale of thy sorowe. And lady am gyuen to be thy sone that am not worthy to be thy seruaunt. For Iohan may not be lykened to Ihesu the sone of Zebede to the sone of god ne the seruaunt to his lorde ne the dyscyple of his mayster ne no creature may be lykened to hym that made hy~. But neuertheles my reuere~de lady shall worshyp you in all that can with all my strength serue you with these wordes and many other wordes Iohn~ comforted me oftentymes euer was redy me to please vnto the resurreccyon of my swete sone Ihesu. And whan we sawe hym aryse from deth to lyfe than were we fulfylled wt more Ioye than they were before with sorowe. And thus endeth our ladyes lamentacyon with grete Ioye of goddes resurreccyon he graunt vs al his benedyccyon. Enprinted at London in Fletestrete at the sygne of the sonne by Wynkyn de Worde.
