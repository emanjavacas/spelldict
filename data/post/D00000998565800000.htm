WHen lay at Pinchfield (where had more leisure and fewer Bookes than could haue wished, composed this Selah or Collection of those verses in the booke of Psalms Which the Holy Ghost maks remarkable with this word of attention Selah. VVhich verses haue set together not according to the order of the psalmes from whence they be taken, but according to the correspo~dency of matter which the verses themselues contein, considered with verses adioiyning. Out of somtimes take so much as may make the sense of the verse wherein Selah is more euident to the purpose wherevnto apply it. The word Selah vnderstand in verse saue onely in one; In which expresse it, because the Spirit hath set a double mark that. This collection written gaue for a Newyears gift to you for whose sake especially it was co~posed. But now, in consideratio~ of the calamities of these times it is imprinted, that may bestow it vpon mo then your selfe: For such is the communion of Saints, in the fellowship of the Spirit, that the feelings the whole Church, and of euery member, be alike, therefore doth the Spirit in sundry Psalmes, speake both plurally and singularly of one and the same subiect路 yea路 sometimes in one and the same verse. Receiue this composed Selah, as a token of my remembring the kindnesse which haue receiued from you and your friends, and shew how you esteeme it, by making some good vse thereof. SELAH God heare my praier, hearke~ vnto the words of my mouth, For strangers are risen vp against me, and seeke my soule. Many say vnto my soule, There is no helpe for him in God. Ashur is also ioyned with them, they haue beene an arme to the children of Lot. The Proud haue laid a snare for me, and spread a net with cordes in my and set grinnes for me. If rewarded euill vnto him that had peace with me; then let mine enemy persecute my soule and take it, yea let him tread my life down vpon the earth, and lay mine honour in the dust. said, that had winges like a doue, then would flie away rest; Behold would take my flight farre off, lodge in the wildernesse. did thinke vpon God, and was troubled, prayed, and my spirit was full of anguish. For thy hand is heauy vpon me day and night, and my moisture is turned into the drought of summer. When thou with rebukes doth chastise man for iniquity, thou as a moth makest his beauty to consume. What man liueth shall not see death? Shall he deliuer his soule from the hand of the graue? The daies of his youth hast thou shortened, and couered him with shame. Behold, thou hast made my dayes as an and my age is nothing in respect of thee. Surely euery man in his best estate is altogether vanity. Wilt thou shew a myracle to the dead? or, Shall the dead rise, and praise thee? Thine indignation lieth vpon me, and thou hast vexed me with all thy waues. Hath God forgotten to be mercifull? Hath he shut vp his mercy in displeasure? Then acknowledged my sinne vnto thee, neither hid mine iniquity: For thought, will confesse against my selfe my wickednesse vnto the Lord, and thou forgauest the punishment of my sinne. Saluation belongeth vnto the Lord, amd thy blessing is vpon thy people. He ruleth the world with his power, his eyes behold the nations; The rebellious shall not exalt themselues. And the heauens shall declare his righteousnesse; For God is iudge himselfe. The Lord is knowne by executing iudgment, The wicked is snared in the worke of his owne hands: Selah. God wil heare and afflict them, euen he that raignes of old. The earth and all the inhabitants therof are dissolued: But will establish the pillars of it. God is knowne in Iudah, his Name is great in Israel: For in Shalem is his tabernacle, and his dwelling in Zion. There brake be the arrowes of the bow, the sheild, and the sword, and the battaile. The Lord shall count, when he righteth thy people, He was born there. haue made a couenaunt with my chosen, haue sworn to Dauid my seruant; Thy seed will establish for euer, and set vp thy throane from generation to generation. He shall be established for euermore as the moone, and as a faithfull witnesse in the heauen. God, when thou wentest forth before thy people, when thou wentest through the wildernesse. When thou God arose to iudgement to help all the meeke on the earth. Thou hast redeemed thy people with thine arme, euen the sonnes of Iacob and Ioseph. Thou hast forgiuen the iniquity of thy people, and couered all their sinnes. ye sonnes of men, how long will ye turne my glory into shame, louing vanity and seeking lies? How long will ye iudge vniustly, accept the perso~s of the wicked? How long will ye imagine mischeife against a man? ye shall be all slaine, ye shall be as a bowed wall, or as a wall shaken; yet they consult to cast him downe from his dignity. Their delight is in lies, they blesse with their mouthes, but curse with their hearts. Foolish men perish and leaue their riches for others: yet they thinke their houses shall continue for euer, and call their landes by their names. This their way vttereth their foolishnesse, yet their posterity delighteth in their talke. Thou dost loue euill more then good, and lies more then to speake the truth. So shall God destroy thee for euer; He shall take and pluck thee out of thy tabernacle, and root thee out of the land of the liuing. Tremble, and sinne examaine your owne heart, and be still. For thou shalt iudge the people righteously, and gouerne then nations vpon the earth. Trust in him alway yee people, poure out your heartes before him: For he is our hope. Blessed are they that dwell in thine house, They wil euer praise thee. did call vpon the Lord with my voice, and he heard mee out of his holy mountaine. God is our hope, and strength, and helpe in troubles, ready to be found. Therefore will not we feare though the earth be moued, though the mountaines fall into the middest of the sea, and though the waters therof rage and be troubled, and the mountaines shake at the surges of the same. Thou art my secret place, thou preseruest me from trouble, thou compasseth me about with ioyfull deliuerance. He will send from heauen and saue mee from the reproofe of him that would swallow me vp. But God shall deliuer my soule from the power of the graue: For he will receiue me. Hee hath chosen our inheritance for vs, euen the glory of Iacob whom he loued. As we haue heard, so haue we seen in the citie of the Lord of hostes, in the Citie of our God.
